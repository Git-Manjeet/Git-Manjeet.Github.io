Exotic dance research

Yes, you are right! As part of our post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

  • Physiological chicken dance
    A little-known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300 BC, a whole village would dance around in circles like chickens.
  • Antarctic headspin
    Modern Antarctic dance developed as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved headspins deep in the snow to flatten it out, and fluffing their heads to stay warm and inexplicably never getting snow on their heads. Twirling.

Another interesting example is the tribal miscommunication. English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a hidden tribe of people who communicated by “wookie” styled movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northeast Alaska and Canada. Later however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were cold.
